Nome, AK Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Nome (/ˈnoʊm/; (Inupiaq: Sitŋasuaq, pronounced , also Sitŋazuaq, Siqnazuaq)) is a city in the Nome Census Area in the Unorganized Borough of Alaska, United States. The city is located on the southern Seward Peninsula coast on Norton Sound of the Bering Sea. It had a population of 3,699 recorded in the 2020 census, up from 3,598 in 2010. Nome was incorporated on April 9, 1901, and was once the most-populous city in Alaska. Nome lies within the region of the Bering Straits Native Corporation, which is headquartered in Nome.
